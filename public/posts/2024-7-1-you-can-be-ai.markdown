You Can Be AI

It's been more than a year since I last posted and for longer than that AI has been infiltrating industry after industry.
It started at chatbots and slowly proved to an insane proportion of the population that it's viable for creating art,
videos, music, and pretty much any piece of content, at this point.
The acceptance AI has earned by the ruthless efforts of its corporate creators is unbelievable.

I have experienced two primary impressions that people have of AI:

- AI is a useful tool. It's safe, and there's no need to uproot it.

- AI is a scourge on the internet.

I feel that AI enables the general public to produce slop without the person even intending to do so.
Every moderately popular website with the ability to post became a breeding ground for AI slop.
Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube are giant culprits.
Though comment/reply bots existed before AI, AI has made it almost effortless and absolutely fueled the creation of new (more) human-like botnets.

I'm reminded of the effect that Unity had on the gaming industry--its ease of use yielded slop.
You could spend a few minutes watching a tutorial and have a running game.
No engine before was so simple.
A lot of kids and overeager devs pushed their games to places like itch.io and gamejolt where it was free to publish games.
Tons of lame horror games, joke games, and genuine low effort games smeared the reputation of Unity, and most regarded it as the engine of bad games.
Those people made those games, though. They were still slop.

People pay creatives because people recognize their effort.
Places like Patreon enable people to pretend like they're artists for knowing how to use an AI program. And by that, I mean knowing how to type in a box.
Typing a description of what the customer wants is literally less work than the customer performed by searching for the AI artist's page.
I don't understand why these insist on the term "AI artist" as opposed to "AI prompter".
They did not make anything. Generating art under your name is analogous to commissioning another artist and acting like you made it.
Those who claim "The art would not have been made had I not provided the description" are affected by a flawed view of the artistic process.
If I am asked to make some art, and I do it, that person is indeed the reason for the art's existence, but they sure as hell aren't the one who made it.

They asked me to. I did it.

The computer was asked. It did it.

If you're ever considering paying for AI-generated art, never do it. Pay a few cents (or nothing!) for the generator they used, and enter your prompt. A few cycles and you'll have an image you like more.

There is a right way to use AI. You can use it to supplant a human-powered goal.
If you have specific needs for some app and you know the basics, it's possible for an AI to code you the rest of the way.
That's fine.

Even if you have no pride in good work, and even if you don't value human work at all, AI hurts you.
All genuine content, including that which has existed beforehand, drowns in sludge today. So you are forced to search longer for things that
are interesting or engaging. You need to try harder to seem human. The popular things you like are being cloned to try and trick you.

AI will never stop. But now that the uninformed public is its own botnet, we need a way to strip the web of steaming garbage.
